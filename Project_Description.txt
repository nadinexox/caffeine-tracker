As finals season approaches, caffeine and energy drinks are unfortunately are big part of my life giving me the extra push to study and complete my work during this stressful time. When trying to figure out a project idea, I took a sip of my RedBull when  I noticed a recurring pattern: I would drink an energy drink to boost my focus, but I couldn't quite predict when that boost would turn into a "crash" and I get tired again. I wanted to see exactly how much caffeine is in my system at any given time and how that correlates with my ability to stay focused.


After some research, caffeine has a half-life of approximately 5 hours. Without a tracking system, it is easy to:
- Over consume caffeine and experience jitters.
- Under consume and still feel tired.
- Fail to account for the metabolic decay of previous drinks.

I made a Python-based tracking system that treats caffeine consumption as a dynamic decay model.

I organized the project into a modular structure given to us as an example to use to ensure the code was easy to navigate and organized:

- Data Logging - I tracked my caffeine intake in a Google Sheet for about a week and turned it into a CSV to be used as a dataframe

- my_module/classes.py: Contains the CaffeineTracker class, which all the drink info, calculates my current caffeine level, allows for easy initialization of the data.

- my_module/functions.py: This contains a lot of the math functions in this project.

- my_script.py: This is a program I can run at the end to quickly type in a new drink (like "Alani Nu, 200mg") and save it to my CSV and dataframe without having to go in and edit it.

- Final_Project.ipynb: This is the main file where I use the classes and functions.
